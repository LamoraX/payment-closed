Welcome to NIBS Workshops 2026

Dear Colleagues,

The Department of Psychiatry at NIMHANS, Bengaluru in collaboration with the Neuromodulation Society of India and Indian Psychiatric Society Neuromodulation Speciality Section is organizing comprehensive workshops on Non-Invasive Brain Stimulation (NIBS).

These workshops cover the fundamentals of:

  • ECT (Electroconvulsive Therapy)
  • TMS (Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation)
  • tES (Transcranial Electrical Stimulation)

Participants will receive hands-on experience in these brain stimulation techniques under expert supervision, facilitating skill development in Indian psychiatrists in this emerging field.

Workshop Objective

The NIBS workshop aims to provide hands-on training in NIBS procedures for practicing psychiatrists. The goal is to enhance their confidence in delivering these treatments effectively and safely.

Additionally, the workshop seeks to develop skilled professionals in this emerging field, contributing to the growth of NIBS expertise and manpower in the country.

What Participants Will Gain

Introductory Knowledge

Comprehensive overview of ECT, tES, and TMS fundamentals and mechanisms.

Practical Aspects

In-depth understanding of the technical and clinical aspects of NIBS procedures.

Hands-on Experience

Direct experience administering NIBS techniques under expert supervision.

Expert Interaction

Engage with leading professionals, discuss clinical applications, and clarify doubts.

Neuromodulation Services at NIMHANS

NIMHANS offers a comprehensive range of non-invasive brain stimulation (NIBS) services, with Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT), Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS), and Transcranial Electrical Stimulation (tES) being routinely utilized for both clinical and research purposes.

In addition to these well-established modalities, the institute is actively conducting research on other advanced neuromodulation techniques, including Transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation (tACS), Transcranial Random Noise Stimulation (tRNS), Transcutaneous Vagal Nerve Stimulation (tVNS), and Photobiomodulation.

Advanced Centre Leadership

The Advanced Centre for Neuromodulation Therapy and Research at NIMHANS is headed by Dr. Jagadisha Thirthalli, with key faculty members including Dr. Venkatasubramanin G, Dr. Muralidharan K., Dr. Preeti Sinha, Dr. Shyamsundar A., Dr. Urvakhsh Mehta, Dr. V.S. Sreeraj, Dr. Jayant Mahadevan, Dr. Narasimha, and Dr. Srinivas B.

Clinical Statistics & Impact

Key Clinical Numbers

  • ECT: More than 7,000 patients treated annually. One of the busiest ECT centers worldwide.
  • TMS: Seven TMS devices (including two deep TMS and MRI-guided Neuronavigation systems). Nearly 2,000 sessions in 2023-2024.
  • tES: 1,501 tES sessions for 90 patients; 145 HD-tES sessions for 10 patients (2023-2024).

Clinical Facilities

Research & Training Excellence

Over the years, eight postdoctoral fellows in NIBS have completed their training at NIMHANS and are currently contributing significantly to clinical practice and research. The NIBS team has also supervised two research training fellowships (2-year duration) and seven PhD thesis (3-5 years duration). The institute has trained 300+ psychiatrists in ECT, TMS, and tES through specialized workshops over the last decade.

Centre for Research in Neuromodulation

In 2021, the Centre for Research in Neuromodulation and Interventional Psychiatry (CRC) was established as a collaborative initiative between NIMHANS Bengaluru, Central Institute of Psychiatry (Ranchi), and Kasturba Medical College (Manipal) funded by DBT Wellcome India Alliance.

Key research initiatives include ECT for clozapine-resistant schizophrenia, clinical trials involving tES and Theta Burst Stimulation (TBS) for hallucinations, and treatment-resistant depression management.
